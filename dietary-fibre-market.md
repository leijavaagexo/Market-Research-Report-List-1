<p><ul><li>Water Soluble Dietary Fiber</li><li>Water Insoluble Dietary Fiber</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dietary fiber market is primarily divided into two types: water-soluble and water-insoluble dietary fibers. Water-soluble fibers dissolve in water, forming a gel-like substance that can help regulate blood sugar levels and lower cholesterol. They are commonly found in oats, beans, and fruits. In contrast, water-insoluble fibers do not dissolve in water and add bulk to the digestive system, promoting regularity and preventing constipation. They are typically sourced from whole grains and vegetables, supporting overall gut health.</p></p>
