<p><p>The Human Immunoglobulin market is primarily segmented into two types: intramuscular injection and intravenous injection. Intramuscular injection delivers immunoglobulin directly into the muscle, facilitating a slower absorption and prolonged action, suitable for outpatient settings. In contrast, intravenous injection administers immunoglobulin directly into the bloodstream, providing rapid therapeutic effects, and is often used in clinical settings for immediate treatment of immune deficiencies. Both methods play crucial roles in managing various infectious and autoimmune disorders, catering to diverse patient needs.</p></p>
