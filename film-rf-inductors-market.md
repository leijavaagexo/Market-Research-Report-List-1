<p><p>Film RF inductors are categorized into low-frequency and high-frequency markets based on their operational characteristics. Low-frequency inductors typically operate below 1 MHz, focusing on audio, power supply, and general signal applications, where larger inductance values are required. In contrast, high-frequency inductors operate in the megahertz to gigahertz range, used in RF communication, wireless devices, and high-speed circuits, demanding compact size and low parasitic capacitance to ensure signal integrity at elevated frequencies. Both markets cater to distinct technological needs.</p></p>
