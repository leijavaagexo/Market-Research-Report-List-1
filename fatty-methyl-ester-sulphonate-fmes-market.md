<p><p>Fatty Methyl Ester Sulphonate (FMES) is a biodegradable anionic surfactant derived from natural fats and oils, primarily used in detergents and cleaning products. The FMES market is categorized into two main processes: esterification and sulphonation. Esterification involves the reaction of fatty acids with methanol to form methyl esters, while sulphonation adds sulfonic acid groups to create the active surfactant. Both processes are crucial for producing FMES, catering to the demand for eco-friendly and effective cleaning solutions.</p></p>
