<p><p>Deposition sources in the market for thin film manufacturing include various techniques such as magnetron sputtering cathodes, thermal evaporation, and electron beam evaporation. Magnetron sputtering cathodes utilize plasma to eject particles from a target material, allowing for high-quality films on substrates. Thermal evaporation involves heating materials until they vaporize, depositing films as they cool. Electron beam evaporation uses focused electron beams to melt materials in vacuum, providing precise control over film properties. Other methods encompass diverse specialized techniques for specific applications.</p></p>
