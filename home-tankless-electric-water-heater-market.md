<p><p>The home tankless electric water heater market comprises two main types: whole-house and point-of-use. Whole-house units are designed to supply hot water for an entire residence, providing a continuous flow and eliminating the need for a storage tank. They are ideal for larger households with significant hot water demand. In contrast, point-of-use heaters are smaller and install directly at the water outlet, supplying hot water for specific fixtures, such as sinks or showers, thereby improving efficiency and reducing energy costs.</p></p>
