<p><p>Terahertz spectroscopy operates across three frequency ranges: low frequency, intermediate frequency, and high frequency, catering to various applications. Low frequency Terahertz spectroscopy focuses on longer wavelengths, suitable for examining bulk material properties. Intermediate frequency offers enhanced resolution and sensitivity, ideal for characterizing biological samples and pharmaceuticals. High frequency Terahertz spectroscopy delivers the most precise measurements, enabling detailed analysis of nanomaterials and thin films, thus driving advancements in research, telecommunications, and material science across diverse industries.</p></p>
