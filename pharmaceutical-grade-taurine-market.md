The Ethylene Oxide Method involves reacting ethylene oxide with taurine precursors, producing high-purity taurine suitable for medical applications. In contrast, the Ethanol Amine Method uses ethanolamine as a catalyst, offering a different chemical pathway that may result in varying purity levels and properties. Both methods aim to meet stringent regulatory standards in the production of taurine for pharmaceuticals and nutraceuticals.</p></p>
