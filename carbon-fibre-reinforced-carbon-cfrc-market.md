<p><p>Carbon Fibre Reinforced Carbon (CFRC) is a composite material known for its outstanding thermal resistance, lightweight properties, and mechanical strength. The CFRC market is primarily divided into two production methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD), which involves depositing carbon onto a substrate through gas-phase reactions, resulting in a dense and uniform material. The Liquid Impregnation Process involves soaking carbon fibers in a liquid resin, providing structural integrity and enhanced performance. Both methods cater to high-temperature applications, including aerospace and automotive industries.</p></p>
