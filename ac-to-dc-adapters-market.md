<p><p>The AC-to-DC adapter market is characterized by two primary connection types: center positive (+) and center negative (-). Center positive connectors have the positive voltage at the center pin, commonly used for devices like laptops and many electronics, ensuring correct polarity for proper functioning. Conversely, center negative connectors place the negative voltage at the center, often found in various audio equipment and older electronics. Both types cater to diverse consumer needs, influencing product compatibility and efficiency in powering devices.</p></p>
