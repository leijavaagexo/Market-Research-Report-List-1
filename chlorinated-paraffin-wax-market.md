<p><p>Chlorinated Paraffin Wax (CPW) is categorized into three main grades based on chlorine content and application: L-grade, M-grade, and H-grade. </p><p>L-grade is low in chlorine, primarily used as a plasticizer and for lubricating applications. M-grade contains a medium level of chlorine, making it suitable for coatings, metalworking fluids, and rubber products. H-grade has a high chlorine content, enhancing flame retardancy, and is commonly used in wire and cable insulation, as well as in various industrial applications.</p></p>
