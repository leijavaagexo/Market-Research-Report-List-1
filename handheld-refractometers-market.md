<p><p>The handheld refractometers market can be categorized into two main types: digital and analog. Digital refractometers use electronic sensors to provide precise readings and often feature enhanced functionalities such as temperature compensation and data storage. In contrast, analog refractometers, or traditional models, rely on optical components and manual calibration to measure the refractive index. Both types serve various industries, including food and beverage, pharmaceuticals, and agriculture, enabling efficient analysis of liquid samples for quality control and formulation.</p></p>
